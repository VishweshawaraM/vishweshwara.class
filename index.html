The Gurukula Way

The Gurukula tradition is not a curriculum delivered in modules. It is a relationship — between student and teacher — built on trust, discipline, and oral transmission.

Vishweshwara studied for more than 7 years in this living system — learning not just the texts but the method of transmission itself. This is what he brings to each student.

"I do not teach for certificates or marks. I teach because this tradition must continue — one student, one session at a time."

गु
Oral Transmission

Texts learned through listening, imitation, and correction — exactly as the tradition has preserved them for millennia.

रु
One-to-One Attention

No group classes. No recorded videos. Each student receives the Acharya's full and undivided attention in every session.

कु
Shankara Bhashya

Bhagavad Gita taught through Adi Shankaracharya's commentary — the definitive Advaita Vedanta interpretation.

ल
Structured Discipline

Fixed schedule, clear curriculum, consistent progress. Committed, serious study — not casual exploration.

Sacred Text · For All Ages

Bhagavad Gītā

गीता सुगीता कर्तव्या

"The Bhagavad Gita should be well sung, well studied." This is not merely a book — it is a living conversation between the Divine and the seeker, set on the field of Kurukshetra, meant for all fields of life.

गीता
For Children

Kathā Style Learning

बालपाठ

Children learn best through narrative. The Bhagavad Gita lives inside the Mahabharata — one of the greatest stories ever told. Vishweshwara uses this richness to make each shloka memorable and alive.

  • Memorising shlokas with correct pronunciation and rhythm
  • Meaning explained through Mahabharata stories
  • Character building — Arjuna's courage, Krishna's wisdom
  • Ethical values drawn from the text and lived examples
  • Age-appropriate pacing with patience and encouragement
  • Regular recitation practice builds confidence and memory
For Adults

Life Solutions & Shankara Bhashya

जीवनमार्ग

The adult programme goes deep — context, philosophy, and real-life application. Taught through Adi Shankaracharya's Bhashya, the definitive non-dual Advaita interpretation of the text.

  • Context of the Mahabharata — why Kurukshetra is universal
  • Why Arjuna's crisis is every human being's crisis
  • Structure and significance of all 700 shlokas across 18 chapters
  • Shankara Bhashya — Advaita Vedanta interpretation in full
  • Karma Yoga, Jnana Yoga, Bhakti Yoga — differences and unity
  • Practical application: clarity, equanimity, and purpose
The Text

700 Shlokas — Complete Teaching

सम्पूर्ण

The Gita's 700 shlokas are studied in sequence and in relation — each chapter answering a question, building toward the final teaching of Chapter 18.

  • 18 chapters — thematic structure made clear from the start
  • Three Yogas — Karma, Jnana, Bhakti — in full depth
  • The Vishvarupa of Chapter 11 — its cosmological significance
  • Chapter 18 — Sarva Dharman parityajya, the final word
  • Connection to Upanishads and Brahma Sutras (advanced)
The Language of Refinement

Sanskrit

संस्कृतं नाम दैवी वाक्

Sanskrit is called the language of the gods — not because it is ancient, but because of its extraordinary precision. Every word is structured, every syllable has a reason. It is the language of refined thought.

सं
Foundation

Pronunciation & Script

उच्चारण

Correct pronunciation in Sanskrit is not merely aesthetic — a wrong accent can change the meaning entirely. This is where every student begins, regardless of prior exposure.

  • Devanagari script — reading and writing with full confidence
  • Complete Sanskrit phoneme system
  • Correct articulation — place of articulation for each sound
  • Svara and vyanjana — vowels, consonants, semi-vowels
  • Reading Sanskrit texts aloud with accuracy and rhythm
Grammar

Pāṇinian Vyākaraṇa

व्याकरण

Grammar taught not as an obstacle but as a key — the key that unlocks the meaning of every text. Taught in traditional sequence with scriptural examples throughout.

  • Vibhakti — all 8 cases, their meanings and usage
  • Sandhi — vowel, consonant, and visarga rules
  • Samāsa — Tatpurusha, Dvandva, Bahuvrīhi compound formation
  • Dhātu — verb roots and conjugation across tenses
  • Krit and Taddhita — word derivation and formation
Advanced

Scriptural Sanskrit

शास्त्रपाठ

The aim is not conversational fluency but the ability to sit with a primary text and read it directly, without depending on translation. The student becomes genuinely independent.

  • Reading Bhagavad Gita shlokas in Sanskrit directly
  • Upanishad passages — Isha, Kena, Katha
  • Classical literature — Ramayana, Kalidasa
  • Shankaracharya's commentary — introductory reading
  • Complete independence in scriptural study
Oral Tradition · Unbroken Lineage

Veda Chanting

मन्त्रः ब्रह्म इति स्मृतः

The Vedas are not read — they are heard, and reproduced with perfect fidelity. The oral tradition has preserved these texts across millennia without a syllable changing. This is what Vishweshwara transmits.

वेद
The Svara System

Udātta · Anudātta · Svarita

स्वर

The three-pitch Vedic accent system distinguishes Vedic Sanskrit from Classical Sanskrit. Without correct svara, chanting loses its traditional character. This is the foundation of every session.

  • Udātta — high pitch: understanding when and how
  • Anudātta — low pitch: context and recognition
  • Svarita — falling tone: types in the Taittirīya tradition
  • Personal correction in every single session — no exceptions
Programme

27-Portion Curriculum

पाठ्यक्रम

The curriculum follows the traditional Gurukula sequence. Each portion builds on the last. Students do not choose what to learn — the Acharya determines the pace and progression based on readiness and correctness.

  • Dasha Shanti Mantras — the foundation
  • Gaṇeśātharvashīrṣam and Gaṇapati Suktam
  • Śrī Suktam, Medha Suktam, Durga Suktam
  • Purusha Suktam and Nārāyaṇa Suktam
  • Śrī Rudram — Namakam and Chamakam
  • Mahānarāyaṇa Upaniṣad select portions
The Lineage

Krishna Yajurveda

परम्परा

Vishweshwara was trained in the Krishna Yajurveda — specifically the Taittirīya tradition of Karnataka. One of the most complete and well-preserved Vedic traditions in India today.

  • Taittirīya tradition — Karnataka Gurukula lineage
  • Rhythm, pace, and phrasing according to living tradition
  • Not a modern or simplified version — full traditional method
  • Śikṣā — precise phonetic rules taught and enforced

Guru Dakṣiṇā

The traditional offering — a student's expression of gratitude

दक्षिणा

What is Guru Dakṣiṇā?

In the Gurukula tradition, Guru Dakṣiṇā is not a fee. It is an offering — a student's acknowledgment that they have received something of immeasurable value. It honours the teacher's time, lineage, and lifelong commitment to the tradition.

श्रद्धा
ŚRADDHĀ

Faith is the foundation. Dakṣiṇā is offered with genuine gratitude for the teaching received — not as a transaction, but as a recognition of immeasurable value.

सेवा
GURŪSEVĀ

Service to the teacher was the original Dakṣiṇā. In the modern context, financial contribution supports the Acharya's time and continued study.

कृतज्ञता
KṚTAJÑATĀ

Gratitude — for the patience, the knowledge, the tradition that reaches you through your teacher. Dakṣiṇā says: I value what I have received.
