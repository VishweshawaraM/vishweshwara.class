Frequently Asked

Is this suitable for complete beginners?+
Absolutely. Most students begin with zero knowledge of Sanskrit or the Gītā. The Acharya assesses your level in the trial session and builds a completely personalised learning path for you.
How is this different from YouTube or an app?+
Every session is live, one-to-one, and deeply personal. Your pronunciation is corrected in real time, your pace is respected, and your questions are answered directly. This is the Gurukula method — a living relationship with the knowledge.
My child is 8 — can they join Gītā classes?+
Yes. The children's programme is designed for ages 6–16 with story-based teaching, age-appropriate explanations, and verse memorisation. Many parents report their children genuinely look forward to every session.
I live abroad — will the timings work?+
The 3:00–4:00 PM IST evening slot works well for UAE (11:30 AM), Singapore (6:30 PM), and USA East (5:30 AM). Mention your timezone when booking and the Acharya will confirm the best fit.
Why is the price exactly ₹500 — always?+
The Precision 500 principle: ₹500 per session, no exceptions, no packages, no confusion. One hour of the Acharya's undivided attention. The simplicity is intentional — it respects the student's intelligence and the teacher's commitment.
What is Guru Dakṣiṇā and is it mandatory?+
Guru Dakṣiṇā is the ancient offering of gratitude from student to teacher — not a fee, but a sacred act. After booking your class, you will be invited to offer Dakṣiṇā. It is not mandatory, but it is honoured deeply.

The Teacher's Heart · The Vision

Why I Teach

A personal reflection from Acharya Vishweshwara.

विद्या
Why I Teach
I did not choose to teach. Teaching chose me — through seven years of living the Gurukula. When you wake before dawn for Veda chanting, when you eat, sleep, and breathe Sanskrit and Vedānta in the same walls where it was taught for generations, something changes in you. You begin to understand that this knowledge is not yours to keep. It belongs to the student who will come next.
I have seen what happens when this tradition is broken. People read translations, listen to YouTube videos, and mistake information for understanding. The Svara disappears from the Veda. The commentary of Śaṅkara is replaced by personal opinions. The living thread becomes a text in a library. I teach because I have been given that living thread — and I cannot let it go dark.
Every student I teach — whether a child in Bangalore learning their first Gītā verse, or an engineer in Singapore rediscovering their roots — is continuing a transmission that is older than writing itself. That responsibility is not something I take lightly. It is the reason I show up, every session, completely present.
The Vision
सर्वे भवन्तु सुखिनः

My vision is simple: that every person of Indian heritage — wherever in the world they live — should have access to this living tradition. Not the edited version. Not the popularised version. The actual thing, as it was transmitted in the Gurukula, with correct Svara, correct grammar, and correct understanding.

The internet has made geography irrelevant. A child in New York can learn the Gītā exactly as a child in Bangalore would. An NRI in Dubai can hear the Veda chanted with the same precision as at Veda Vijnana Gurukulam. That is the miracle of this age — and I intend to use it fully.

The Gurukula gave me knowledge for free, sustained by generations of support and devotion. Teaching is my way of repaying that gift — not to the institution, but to the tradition itself.

— Acharya Vishweshwara M · Veda Vijnana Gurukulam · 2025
